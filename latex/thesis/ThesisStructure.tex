\begin{abstract}

  In recent years open-source software (OSS) adoption has become more widespread than ever before. As more organizations rely on OSS solutions, there is an increased need for understanding how developers self-organize to maintain and improve the projects. This paper fills the state-of-the-art literature gap by examining the developer collaboration network (DCN) in OSS software projects using temporal models. We investigate how the DCN changes due to external and internal events. First, we select a handful of projects to conduct a detailed empirical analysis; then, we compose a randomized sample of 110 OSS repositories to generalize our observations on a large scale. Despite the heterogeneity of OSS projects, we found that the DCN becomes more chaotic around new major and minor releases, which poses several organizational challenges. Further findings and implications are discussed in the paper.

\end{abstract}
